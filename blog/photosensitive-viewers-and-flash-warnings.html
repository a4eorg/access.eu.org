Photosensitive Viewers and Flash Warnings

19 May 2021   Access for Everyone
Home â€¢ Blog

We see a lot of content on the internet with flashing lights, quick transitions, and/or high contrast and saturation. This sort of content can adversely affect photosensitive viewers.

This includes neurodivergent people with sensory issues who can be put into a state of sensory overload. It can also affect individuals with photosensitive epilepsy and cause them to have seizures.

It is common for photosensitive watchers to have migraines in the wake of being presented with this sort of content.

What Can We Do?

Do's

  • Add a flash warning before any post that may affect photosensitive viewers
  • In case of reels, tiktoks and stories, add a flash warning in the first few seconds before the video
  • Make the text bold and visible to your audience
  • Reduce contrast and avoid fully saturated reds
  • Be objective in your descriptions

Don'ts

  • Don't write the flash warning in the caption, instead write it in the first slide of your post
  • Don't keep the flash warning text too small or show it once the video has already started
  • Don't add flickering or flashing images, stickers, filters, GIFs etc

Additional Information

There are certain social media trends that may trigger photosensitive audiences. Some of these are the "20 Photos 2 Videos" trend, and filters/effects available on social media like "the Rainbow Flash".

"Trippy" filters used for aesthetic purposes may contain rapid movements and high contrast & saturation also have the same effect.

Remember: If your content contains anything that flashes more than 3 times in any 1 second, or has movements with high contrast & saturation, add a flash warning.
